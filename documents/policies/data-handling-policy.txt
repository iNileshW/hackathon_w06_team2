Departmental Data Handling Policy for FOI Responses

VERSION 3.2 -- Effective from April 2024

1. CLASSIFICATION
All FOI responses must be handled at OFFICIAL classification unless the underlying information is classified at a higher level. If the requested information is OFFICIAL-SENSITIVE or above, consult the Information Asset Owner before proceeding.

2. PERSONAL DATA
Before releasing any information, check whether it contains personal data as defined under the Data Protection Act 2018. Personal data includes: names, contact details, staff numbers, job titles, performance data, disciplinary records, and any information from which an individual could be identified directly or indirectly.

For requests about staffing data:
- Aggregate statistics (e.g., total headcount by grade) are generally safe to release.
- Individual-level data must be anonymised. Remove names, staff numbers, and any combination of fields that could identify an individual.
- Small sample sizes (fewer than 5 individuals in a category) risk identification even with anonymisation. Consider suppressing these cells or combining categories.

3. COMMERCIAL DATA
Contract values and supplier names for awarded contracts are published proactively and can be released under FOI. However, the following are protected under Section 43:
- Bid evaluation scores and ranking of unsuccessful bidders
- Pricing breakdowns and unit rates
- Supplier profit margins
- Negotiation correspondence between the department and bidders

4. INTERNAL DELIBERATIONS
Emails, meeting notes, and memos reflecting internal policy deliberations may be protected under Section 36. Apply the public interest test:
- Favour disclosure when: the decision has been made and announced, significant public money is involved, or there are concerns about the decision-making process.
- Favour withholding when: disclosure would inhibit candid advice in future, the policy area is still under active deliberation, or disclosure would reveal legal advice.

5. REDACTION STANDARDS
When applying partial disclosure:
- Use solid black redaction bars (not highlighting or strikethrough).
- Ensure redacted text cannot be recovered from the digital file (flatten PDFs, do not use text boxes over content).
- Label each redaction with the applicable exemption section number.
- Produce a redaction schedule listing each redaction, the exemption applied, and a brief explanation.

6. QUALITY ASSURANCE
Every FOI response must be reviewed by a second pair of eyes before release. The reviewer checks:
- All personal data has been identified and handled correctly
- Exemptions are correctly cited with section numbers
- The response is clear and addresses all parts of the request
- The response timeline is being met (20 working days from receipt)
- Any public interest test is documented
